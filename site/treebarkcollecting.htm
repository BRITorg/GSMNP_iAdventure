BARK/LICHEN COLLECTING GUIDE

prepared by Trish Smith, Warrensburg Middle School, 9/04

 

1.  You have the job of collecting bark samples from our study trees.  BE CAREFUL NOT TO HARM THE TREE OR YOURSELF.  If you pry to deeply with your tool, you may damage the living tissue under the bark, allowing disease-causing organisms to enter the tree.  If the bark is attached tightly, you must be careful not to let your tool slip...you do not want to gauge (cut) yourself!

 

2.  Start by looking at all sides of the base of the tree and selecting the side with the most signs of other life forms living on the bark.  Place your 15 cm2  measuring square against the tree close to the ground and on the side with the most growth.  Use your chalk to outline the square.

 

3.  Mark a paper sack with the tree number, date, and "ground".

 

4.  Now, take your prying tool (screwdriver, putty knife) and carefully remove the bark that is inside your chalk line.  Place each piece in the paper sack.

 

5.  Measure up the side of the tree to 3 meters and mark it with chalk.  Again looking at all sides of the tree, select the side with the most signs of other life forms living on the bark.  Place your 15 cm2  measuring square against the tree 3 meters above the ground and on the side with the most growth.  Use your chalk to outline the square.  BE VERY CAREFUL WHILE ON THE STEPLADDER...BE SURE THAT YOUR GROUNDCREW IS HOLDING THE LADDER STEADY.

 

6. Mark a paper sack with the tree number, date, and "3 METERS".

 

4.  Now, take your prying tool (screwdriver, putty knife) and carefully remove the bark that is inside of this second chalk line.  Place each piece in the paper sack.
